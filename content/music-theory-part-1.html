Staves & Bars

  • A stave is used to write notes
  • Composed of 5 evenly spaced horizontal lines
  • Vertical lines separate different bars (or measures)
  • Every bar has the same number of beats

Tempo & Rhythm

  • Tempo describes the steady pulse of the music
  • Rhythm describes the variation of length between different notes

Tempo

  • Tempo describes the steady pulse of the music
  • Described using terms like adagio (slow) or allegro (fast)
  • Beats per minute (BPM) give a more accurate description

  • The treble (or G) clef symbol is 

  • The vertical position of the clef defines the line for the note

Ties & Slurs

  • Ties join notes that sound the same into one
  • Slurs, smooth transition between different notes (legato)


Accidentals

  • Sharps () raise the tone by half-step
  • Flats () lower the tone by half-step
  • Natural () cancels any sharps or flats
  • Accidentals are placed before the note on the stave
  • And affect the same notes until the end of the bar (or measure)


Major Scales

  • A scale is any predefined series of notes
  • The distance between notes defines the tonality of the scale
  • The distance can be a whole tone (T) or a semitone (S)
  • Major scales pattern is T-T-S-T-T-T-S
  • Example for the C Major scale:


CDEFGABC
└T┘└T┘└S┘└T┘└T┘└T┘└S┘

C Major Scale

  • Starts and ends on C
  • Follows the major scale pattern
  • Doesn't require sharps () of flats ()


G Major Scale

  • Starts and ends on G
  • Follows the major scale pattern
  • Requires a F


D Major Scale

  • Starts and ends on D
  • Follows the major scale pattern
  • Requires a F and a C


F Major Scale

  • Starts and ends on F
  • Follows the major scale pattern
  • Requires a B
